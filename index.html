About Me

I’m an undergraduate physics major and current astrophysics researcher at Georgia State University, studying barium dwarfs and white dwarf companions using GAIA data, high-res spectroscopy, and interferometry. I am currently working with Dr. Russel White and CHARA data to understand 40 Eri binary evolution and its potential s-process enrichment.

Additional work with Dr. Xiaochun He includes assembling cosmic muon detectors in a world-wide system to monitor long term space weather. My work this fall involves performing statistical analysis on the effects of tidal forces on cosmic muon flux.

In addition to my research, I’m passionate about science communication and space exploration.

Feel free to explore my site to learn more about my research, projects, and interests. If you’d like to get in touch, please visit the contact section below.
